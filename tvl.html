Technical Vocational Livelihood

What is TVL?

The TVL (Technical-Vocational-Livelihood) strand is an academic track in
senior high school that focuses on technical skills and vocational training.
It is designed to prepare students for employment in specific industries or
trades directly after graduation.

Offered Tracks

What is the ICT track?

The ICT (Information and Communication Technology) track is an academic track in
senior high school that focuses on developing students' skills and knowledge in
information technology, computer science, and related fields. It aims to prepare
students for careers in the rapidly evolving and technology-driven industry.


What career paths can I pursue after completing the ICT track?

Graduates of the ICT track can pursue careers in various IT-related fields such as
software development, web development, network administration, database management,
cybersecurity, and digital marketing. The track also provides a foundation for higher
education in computer science or related disciplines.


Is the ICT track suitable for students interested in entrepreneurship in the tech industry?

Yes, the ICT track is suitable for students interested in entrepreneurship in the tech industry.
The curriculum often includes subjects related to entrepreneurship, and the technical skills
acquired can be valuable for starting and managing technology-based businesses.

What is H.E track?

The Home Economics track is an academic track in senior high school that focuses on
practical life skills, home management, and related areas. It prepares students for
careers in various fields such as culinary arts, hospitality, caregiving, and entrepreneurship.


What career paths can I pursue after completing the Home Economics track?

Graduates of the Home Economics track can pursue careers in various fields, including
culinary arts, hospitality, caregiving, event planning, and entrepreneurship.
They may work in restaurants, hotels, catering services, or start their own businesses.


Is the Home Economics track suitable for students interested in starting their own businesses?

Yes, the Home Economics track is suitable for students interested in entrepreneurship,
especially in areas such as culinary arts, catering, or home-based businesses.
The track provides practical skills and knowledge that are valuable for entrepreneurial ventures.

What is Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW)?

Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW), also known as stick welding, is a welding process that uses a consumable
electrode coated in flux to join metals. The process is widely used in various industries
for its versatility and ability to weld different types of metals.


What career paths can I pursue after completing the SMAW track?

Graduates of a SMAW program can pursue careers as welders in industries such as construction,
manufacturing, shipbuilding, automotive, and infrastructure development.
Welders may work in fabrication shops, construction sites, or other
environments where metal joining is essential.


Is the SMAW track suitable for students interested in a hands-on and technical career?

Yes, the SMAW track is suitable for students interested in a hands-on and technical career in welding.
The program provides practical skills and knowledge needed for a successful career in the welding industry.

What is the Dressmaking track?

The Dressmaking track is a specialized vocational or technical training program that focuses on developing skills
in garment construction, pattern making, and sewing. It is designed to prepare individuals for careers in the fashion and apparel industry.


What career paths can I pursue after completing the Dressmaking track?

Graduates of the Dressmaking track can pursue careers as dressmakers, tailors, fashion designers, or garment production specialists.
They may work in fashion houses, apparel manufacturing companies, or establish their own dressmaking businesses.


Is the Dressmaking track suitable for students interested in starting their own fashion business?

Yes, the Dressmaking track is suitable for students interested in entrepreneurship in the fashion industry.
The skills acquired in garment construction and design can be valuable for individuals looking to start
their own fashion or dressmaking businesses.
