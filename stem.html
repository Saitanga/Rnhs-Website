Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics

What Is Stem?

The STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) strand is an academic
track in high school that focuses on preparing students for careers in science and
-related fields. It emphasizes a comprehensive and interdisciplinary approach to learning,
fostering critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and hands-on experiences.
Students in the STEM strand engage in rigorous coursework that includes advanced
mathematics, science subjects, and technology-oriented subjects, laying the foundation
for future pursuits in fields such as engineering, computer science, medicine, and other STEM-related disciplines.


What career paths does the STEM strand lead to?

The STEM strand opens doors to a wide range of career paths, including but not limited to engineering,
computer science, medicine, research, data science, and technology-related fields. Graduates often pursue
professions that require a strong foundation in science, technology, engineering, or mathematics.


Is the STEM strand suitable for students who are not naturally inclined towards mathematics or science?

While a basic aptitude and interest in mathematics and science can be beneficial,
the STEM strand is designed to develop these skills in students. With dedicated teaching methods,
practical applications, and hands-on experiences, students can improve their proficiency and understanding
of STEM subjects even if they initially find them challenging.
