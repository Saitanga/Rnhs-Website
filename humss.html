Humanities and Social Sciences

What is HUMSS?

The HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences) strand is an academic track offered in
senior high schools that focuses on developing students' knowledge and skills in
the fields of humanities, social sciences, and the arts. This strand is designed
to provide a broad understanding of various disciplines, including literature,
philosophy, communication, political science, psychology, sociology, and anthropology.
HUMSS aims to cultivate critical thinking, effective communication, and a deep
appreciation for culture and societal issues. Students who choose the HUMSS strand
may pursue careers in areas such as journalism, law, education, social work, public
administration, and the arts. The curriculum often includes subjects that explore the
complexities of human experiences, social structures, and cultural phenomena.


What career paths can I pursue after completing the HUMSS strand?

HUMSS graduates can pursue careers in journalism, law, education, social work, psychology,
public administration, international relations, human resources, arts and culture, and research,
among other fields. The strand provides a versatile foundation for various professions.


Is the HUMSS strand suitable for students who are interested in pursuing careers in the arts?

Yes, the HUMSS strand is suitable for students interested in the arts. It includes subjects related to
creative arts, literature, and cultural studies, offering a foundation for those interested in pursuing
careers in the arts and cultural sectors.
